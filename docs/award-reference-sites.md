# Award Reference Sites for Fairyteller

Last updated: 2026-05-25

## Search Lens

Target direction:

> editorial boutique commerce + tactile book object + calm personalization + one magical reveal

The best references rarely match all four dimensions. The useful set is a cluster:

- book/object commerce;
- calm guided choice;
- personalization/configuration;
- editorial product storytelling;
- one memorable interactive reveal.

## Best Matches

### 1. MY STELLA - Personalized Book

Awards/sources:

- [Awwwards Nominee](https://www.awwwards.com/sites/my-stella-personalized-book)
- [CSS Design Awards Special Kudos](https://www.cssdesignawards.com/sites/my-stella-personalized-book/47840/)

Why it matters:

- Closest direct reference: personalized picture book where the child is the hero.
- Awwwards tags it with e-commerce, forms/input, interaction design, UI, illustration, storytelling.
- CSSDA describes it as a personalized picture book where users can instantly preview all pages.

Fairyteller takeaway:

- Build a product page/construction flow where personalization is visible and calm.
- Show page preview early, not only after form submission.
- Make "you are the hero" emotional, but keep the flow understandable.

Use carefully:

- It is more children's-brand-coded than Fairyteller should be.

### 2. Mendo Book Store

Awards/sources:

- [Awwwards Site of the Day, Site of the Year 2017](https://www.awwwards.com/sites/mendo-book-store)
- [CSS Design Awards Website of the Day](https://www.cssdesignawards.com/sites/mendo/30387/)

Why it matters:

- Direct book-commerce reference.
- Awwwards describes it as "a candy store for book aficionados".
- Award page highlights shopping cart and e-commerce search filters.

Fairyteller takeaway:

- Treat books as desirable physical objects.
- Product/category browsing can feel editorial and tactile without losing commerce.
- Use black/white or restrained palette if the product imagery carries the mood.

Use carefully:

- It is a bookstore, not a creation flow; it is more useful for examples/catalog sections than the constructor.

### 3. Vitra Gift Finder

Awards/sources:

- [Awwwards Honorable Mention](https://www.awwwards.com/sites/vitra-gift-finder)
- [Build in Amsterdam case study](https://www.buildinamsterdam.com/case/vitra-gift-finder)
- [Lovie Awards coverage](https://lbbonline.com/news/build-in-amsterdam-scores-5-wins-at-2023-lovie-awards/)

Why it matters:

- Very strong gift-selection pattern.
- Build in Amsterdam describes it as an interactive tool that asks the right questions and guides users toward suitable options.
- Lovie coverage says it won in Retail & Shopping and Best User Experience.

Fairyteller takeaway:

- The `/create` flow should feel like a gift finder/order assistant, not a machine config.
- Let users answer a few pleasant questions and see the result become more specific.
- Use a shareable/returnable preview idea for story choices.

Use carefully:

- Vitra's cloud-like product exploration is playful; Fairyteller should keep the interaction simpler on mobile.

### 4. Vitra Office Chair Finder

Awards/sources:

- [Awwwards Site of the Day](https://www.awwwards.com/sites/vitra-office-chair-finder)
- [Awwwards element: answering a few questions](https://www.awwwards.com/inspiration/answering-a-few-questions)
- [Awwwards element: smart search and comparison](https://www.awwwards.com/inspiration/smart-searching-and-comparing-the-results)

Why it matters:

- Excellent example of calm guided personalization.
- Awwwards describes it as a smart, playful tool to find a product by answering questions and comparing results.

Fairyteller takeaway:

- Step labels should be meaningful, not just "1, 2, 3".
- Ask one good question at a time.
- Use recommendation/preview language: "подойдёт для романтического подарка", "лучше для семейной истории".

Use carefully:

- Fairyteller's emotional input is richer than office-chair attributes, so avoid making the flow too utilitarian.

### 5. Secrid

Awards/sources:

- [Build in Amsterdam case study](https://www.buildinamsterdam.com/case/secrid)
- [Awwwards profile listing Secrid as SOTD](https://www.awwwards.com/buildinamsterdam/)
- [Dutch Digital Design case](https://www.dutchdigital.design/cases/secrid)
- [Secrid personalization/engraving tool case](https://www.q42.nl/en/work/secrid-personalise)

Why it matters:

- Tactile product, premium but warm.
- The case explicitly connects offline and online brand worlds.
- The personalization tool is high-quality yet easy to use.

Fairyteller takeaway:

- Digital UI should feel connected to printed book/PDF artifact.
- "Personalization" needs a quiet premium treatment, not gimmick UI.
- The photo/name/story-details flow can feel like engraving: precise, careful, permanent.

Use carefully:

- Secrid is a hardware/accessory brand; borrow the restraint, not the industrial tone.

### 6. Suitsupply Look Builder

Awards/sources:

- [Awwwards Honorable Mention](https://www.awwwards.com/sites/suitsupply-look-builder)
- [Build in Amsterdam case study](https://www.buildinamsterdam.com/case/suitsupply)

Why it matters:

- Strong personalization/configuration reference.
- The case describes "minimum UI, maximum content", digital magazines, personal measurements, and a look builder that generates looks quickly.

Fairyteller takeaway:

- Let images/content do the emotional work; keep controls quiet.
- Store user inputs as a "story passport" idea: heroes, tone, style, delivery.
- Consider a "preview panel" that updates as choices are made.

Use carefully:

- Fashion tone may be too sharp; Fairyteller should be softer.

### 7. Polaroid I-2 / Polaroid.com

Awards/sources:

- [Polaroid I-2 Awwwards Site of the Day](https://www.awwwards.com/sites/polaroid-i-2)
- [Build in Amsterdam profile listing Polaroid.com as Honorable Mention](https://www.awwwards.com/buildinamsterdam/)
- [Aurélien Brigaud Polaroid case](https://aurelien-brigaud.fr/polaroid)

Why it matters:

- Great "analog object in a modern store" reference.
- Awwwards highlights scroll interaction, intro animation, photo gallery, and camera setting exploration.
- Case language is useful: tangible analog feel, restrained visual language, generous space, contact-sheet cues, editorial type, quiet motion.

Fairyteller takeaway:

- We need a tactile print/book feel translated to screen.
- Use a restrained reveal: page turns, cover opening, first-chapter preview appearing.
- Motion should feel human and analog, not synthetic.

Use carefully:

- Do not overdo retro nostalgia.

### 8. Moooi Paper Play

Awards/sources:

- [Awwwards Site of the Day](https://www.awwwards.com/sites/moooi-paper-play)
- [Webby Crafted with Code / Best Visual Design - Aesthetic Winner](https://www.webbyawards.com/crafted-with-code/moooi-paper-play/)
- [Awwwards case study](https://www.awwwards.com/case-study-moooi-paper-play.html)

Why it matters:

- Best reference for "one magical reveal".
- Webby case frames it as chapters/acts that make products shoppable in an unforgettable way.
- Awwwards case notes poetic audio, product sounds, and interactive scenes.

Fairyteller takeaway:

- The generation/preview state can be theatrical: a chapter opens, illustration appears, book title settles.
- Structure the magic as one moment, not constant effects.

Use carefully:

- Moooi is highly experimental; Fairyteller should use 10% of this energy, not 100%.

### 9. Flying Papers

Awards/sources:

- [Awwwards Site of the Day](https://www.awwwards.com/sites/flying-papers)
- [Awwwards case study](https://www.awwwards.com/case-study-flying-papers.html)
- [Webby/LBB coverage](https://lbbonline.com/news/build-in-amsterdam-picks-up-three-webbys-2)

Why it matters:

- Great example of using storytelling and rich animation to sell a difficult physical product without being literal.
- Useful for how to communicate sensitive/technical product constraints through brand world.

Fairyteller takeaway:

- We do not need to show AI internals. Tell the experience through metaphor: book workshop, pages, editorial marks, illustration reveal.
- Use brand characters/elements sparingly as guides.

Use carefully:

- It is too expressive/quirky for Fairyteller's trust-building areas.

### 10. My Little Storybook

Awards/sources:

- [Awwwards Site of the Day](https://www.awwwards.com/sites/my-little-storybook)
- [Awwwards element: immersive children storytelling book](https://www.awwwards.com/inspiration/my-little-storybook-experimental-and-immersive-children-storybook)

Why it matters:

- Strong interactive picture-book reference.
- Shows how to make a story world feel alive through scene transitions, sound, and illustration.

Fairyteller takeaway:

- Useful for chapter preview and generated story reveal, not for the whole commerce page.
- A small read-aloud/book-opening moment could make the preview memorable.

Use carefully:

- WebGL storybook sites often sacrifice commerce clarity and performance.

### 20. Google x BASIC

Awards/sources:

- [Red Dot project](https://www.red-dot.org/project/google-x-basicr-49213)

- The project aimed to replicate physical shopping online and let customers "touch and try" products before buying.

Fairyteller takeaway:

- Let people "touch" the book digitally: open spread, page preview, cover/title, print artifact, PDF preview.
- Integrate comparison/choice without feeling like a control panel.

Use carefully:

- Hardware-product storytelling should be adapted into book/story storytelling.

## What This Means for Fairyteller

### Homepage

Use Mendo/Polaroid/Secrid/BRAUN as the restraint layer:

- real book object first;
- cream/ink editorial palette;
- large product imagery;
- little UI chrome;
- clear examples and proof.

Use Wonderbly/MY STELLA as the personalization layer:

- recipient-first;
- show what changes in the book;
- preview pages early;
- make "hero is you" concrete.

### Constructor

Use Vitra Gift Finder, Vitra Chair Finder, Secrid Personalise, and Suitsupply Look Builder:

- ask one question at a time;
- show a changing preview panel;
- step labels are meaningful;
- choices are visual, but not noisy;
- personal data feels handled with care.

### Preview / Generation Moment

Use Moooi Paper Play, My Little Storybook, Oat the Goat, and Black Dog only as the reveal layer:

- cover/title reveal;
- page opens;
- first illustration fades in;
- chapter excerpt appears like print, not like chat output;
- one small sound/motion idea only if it does not hurt mobile.

## Practical Direction

The target is not an award-site clone. It is:

> A warm, editorial, tactile commerce page where the user feels they are commissioning a real personalized book, then gets one memorable magical preview moment.

The next design test should combine:

1. Mendo/Polaroid book-object hero.
2. MY STELLA personalization clarity.
3. Vitra guided gift flow.
4. Secrid-level craft and care.
5. Moooi/My Little Storybook reveal, used once.
